How we met

Ravi and Sam's story began during Ravi's first week of work for the government, where Sam was assigned as Ravi's onboarding buddy. Just a few weeks later, COVID-19 changed everything, and the world went into lockdown. With both of them working on the same team and project, they found themselves navigating these unusual times together, often being each other's only in-person company. The close connection they formed during those months soon blossomed into something more, and at the end of the year, they began dating.

Once they started dating, they found themselves constantly traveling back and forth between Maryland, where Ravi lived, and DC, where Sam lived, making the most of their weekends together. Eventually, Sam received an exciting opportunity to move to California for work, and without hesitation, Ravi followed. From their initial days as colleagues to becoming each other's partners in adventure, their journey is filled with love, growth, and a sense of togetherness that has only deepened over time. And the rest, as they say, is history.

Travel & Lodging

Below you’ll find information on how to get from the three major New York City airports to Williamsburg, Brooklyn, as well as recommended accommodations for our wedding.

Newark Liberty Airport (EWR)

Public Transit: Approximately 1.5 to 2 hours, costing around $20. Take the AirTrain Newark to NJ Transit, then transfer to the NYC Subway to reach Williamsburg.

Rideshare: Around 1 hour depending on traffic, costing between $60 to $100. Services like Uber or Lyft provide direct transportation to Williamsburg.

LaGuardia International Airport (LGA)

Public Transit: Approximately 1 to 1.5 hours, costing $2.75. Take the Q70-SBS bus to Jackson Heights-Roosevelt Ave station, then transfer to the subway to reach Williamsburg.

Rideshare: Around 30 minutes depending on traffic, costing between $30 to $50. Services like Uber or Lyft provide direct transportation to Williamsburg.

John F. Kennedy Airport (JFK)

Public Transit: Approximately 1 to 1.5 hours, costing around $11. Take the AirTrain JFK to Jamaica Station, then transfer to the subway (J train) to reach Williamsburg.

Rideshare: Around 30 minutes to 1 hour depending on traffic, costing between $35 to $60. Services like Uber or Lyft provide direct transportation to Williamsburg.
